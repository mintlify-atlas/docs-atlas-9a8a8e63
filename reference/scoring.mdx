PA_PVP computes a numeric Decision Score as a triage signal. It is not a truth claim — it drives impact bucket assignment (LOW/MED/HIGH), output scaling, and data acquisition prioritization. The verdict always takes precedence over the score. When the score sits near an impact threshold, the Fragility Gate may move an item into `PROBING` before a verdict is finalized, which is one of the clearest signs that the score is doing its intended job: surfacing instability rather than forcing a premature close.

---

## FULL Profile Scoring

Used for normal, non-DIY items. All four variables are rated LOW=1, MED=2, HIGH=3.

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `B` (Benefit) | Expected upside if the decision succeeds |
| `C` (Cost) | Resource, time, or effort cost of pursuing this item |
| `R` (Risk) | Probability-weighted downside or harm |
| `D` (Cost of Delay) | Value lost by waiting instead of acting now |

**Formula:**

```text
Raw = (B * 1) - (C * 0.8) - (R * 1.2) + (D * 0.5)
Score = ((Raw + 4.5) / 7.0) * 100, clamped 0..100
```

**Impact mapping:**

| Score range | Impact |
|---|---|
| 70 – 100 | HIGH |
| 40 – 69 | MED |
| 0 – 39 | LOW |

The weights are intentional: Risk is penalized more heavily than Cost (`R * 1.2` vs `C * 0.8`), and Cost of Delay is given a positive contribution (`D * 0.5`) to reward time-sensitivity. A decision with high benefit, low cost, low risk, and high cost of delay will score near the top of the range.

## DIY Profile Scoring (Anchor-Based)

Used for fast execution contexts: setup, debug, refactor, quick operations. Variables are rated LOW=1, MED=2, HIGH=3.

| Variable | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `B` (Benefit) | Expected upside |
| `C` (Cost) | Effort or resource cost |
| `R` (Risk) | Downside or harm |
| `D` (Cost of Delay) | Value lost by waiting |
| `Friction` | Operational friction — LOW=1, MED=2, HIGH=3 |

**Formula:**

```text
Raw = (B * 3) - (C + R + D + Friction)
```

**Impact mapping:**

| Raw value | Impact |
|---|---|
| ≥ 4 | HIGH |
| 1 – 3 | MED |
| ≤ 0 | LOW |

**Decision Score display anchors (DIY, display-only):**

| Impact | Anchor score |
|---|---|
| HIGH | 85 |
| MED | 60 |
| LOW | 30 |

**Confidence penalty (visual-only):** If `critical_driver_confidence` is LOW or VERY_LOW, the display anchor is reduced by 20 points (clamped to 0..100). This is a visual signal only — it does not affect impact, triggers, or routing.

<Note>
The DIY Decision Score does **not** drive triggers. Impact drives triggers in the DIY profile. The numeric anchor is display-only and exists only to give a human-readable indication of score magnitude. Governance conditions, gates, and verdicts derive from the `impact` bucket, not from the anchor number.
</Note>

## Fragility Gate

When the score lands near an impact threshold, the item is considered fragile and should not be closed without a probe.

**FULL profile:**

| Fragile range | Near threshold |
|---|---|
| 35 – 45 | Near the 40 (LOW/MED) boundary |
| 65 – 75 | Near the 70 (MED/HIGH) boundary |

**DIY profile:**

Raw in `{0, 1, 3, 4}` — values one step from the MED and HIGH impact boundaries.

**Rule:** If `FRAGILE_SCORE=TRUE` and `impact` is not LOW:
- The item is treated as `PROBING`.
- The kernel prioritizes an internal simulation or probe targeting the `critical_driver`.
- The item is not finalized to `CLOSED` in this run unless the probe resolves the boundary.
- If Cost of Delay is HIGH, a Hedge Action is allowed (with risk cap, parallel probe, and declared `falsification_debt`), but the item remains `PROBING`.
- `trigger=FRAGILITY` is set unless a higher-priority trigger already governs the item.

## Verdict Precedence

The Decision Score is a consistency check and scaling signal, not a decision authority. The verdict follows this precedence order — higher entries override lower ones:

1. **Falsification Principle** — A representative, context-matched probe that falsifies the critical driver forces `verdict=DISCARD` unconditionally.
2. **Reality Probe / Hedge / Truth-or-Dare governance constraints** — Governance rules from active probes, hedge conditions, or truth-or-dare obligations.
3. **Expected net benefit under uncertainty** — Driver-based analysis supported by Evidence Binding. This is where the score contributes most directly.
4. **Score/Impact as consistency check and scaling signal only** — Impact bucket determines output compression and data acquisition priority. It does not override the above.

<Note>
The Decision Score is display-only in the output and may be omitted from `[ITEM_PANEL]`. It is not the sole reason for a verdict — the verdict is always determined by the precedence order above, with the score serving as a triage and scaling tool rather than a decision authority.
</Note>

## Evidence Tiers

Evidence quality directly affects confidence caps and, through them, the score's reliability. Higher-tier evidence allows higher confidence values; lower-tier evidence enforces automatic caps.

| Tier | Description |
|---|---|
| `SIMULATED` | Internally derived by the AI — no external observation; thought experiments, synthetic examples |
| `DERIVED` | Calculated from assumptions within the run — no real measurement |
| `HISTORICAL` | Past observations from external sources, not from the current context |
| `REAL` | Direct observation or measurement in the current operational context |
| `EXPERIMENT` | Controlled experiment or probe result, adversarially designed to falsify a critical driver |

The order from weakest to strongest is: `SIMULATED < DERIVED < HISTORICAL < REAL < EXPERIMENT`.

**Confidence caps by tier and domain:**

| Domain | SIMULATED / DERIVED | HISTORICAL | REAL / EXPERIMENT |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deterministic | MED (unless provably tested) | HIGH allowed | HIGH allowed |
| Probabilistic | MED | MED | HIGH allowed |
| Complex / Chaotic | MED | MED | HIGH (if `representativeness=HIGH`, `context_match=YES`, `contested=NO`) |

When declared confidence exceeds the allowed cap, it is downgraded to the cap value and `trigger=CONFIDENCE_CAP_APPLIED` is set (unless a higher-priority trigger already governs the item).

## Missing Ratings Handling

If any of `rating`, `drivers`, `confidence`, or `evidence_tier` is missing for Benefit, Cost, Risk, or Cost of Delay, the kernel applies defaults rather than blocking:

| Missing field | Default applied |
|---|---|
| `rating` | `MED` |
| `confidence` | `LOW` |
| `evidence_tier` | `SIMULATED` |
| `drivers` | Includes `MISSING_INFO` |

The item is marked `missing_ratings=YES` in `[ITEM_PANEL]`, a top-priority data acquisition entry is added for the missing inputs, and the item is not transitioned to `CLOSED` unless `impact=LOW`. For DIY profile items with missing Friction, the default is `Friction=MED` with `confidence=LOW`.
